</div><h1 id="h_01KG0KZXGBQFQNG1W4C6KT7T07">What Are Video Codecs?</h1><p>Similar to images (ex. jpg, png), there are a variety of technologies for encoding and decoding video. Each video codec has various tradeoffs, but they all have the same goal of storing, transmitting, and playing video efficiently.</p><p>The Discord app uses video codecs in several parts of its experience including: </p><ul>
<li data-list-item-id="e9da9e2c792a11f308caf8cee84aeba90">File attachment playback</li>
<li data-list-item-id="e8b9c6c09f7be9dbddec3d42753d08a7f">Quest videos</li>
<li data-list-item-id="ef2980d73489025c319ba7e911dc7749b">Video calls</li>
<li data-list-item-id="e55c1c8c38828b04ab9e9e8e4f26be48a">Go-Live screenshare</li>
</ul><p>Discord relies on codecs provided by the operating system, such as Windows. When they’re unavailable, parts of the app experience will not function properly. </p><h1 id="h_01KG0M05MZCN973CPZ7X1RSE27">How To Get Video Codecs on Windows</h1><p>Most Windows users already have the necessary packages, but you can follow the steps below for each codec to verify support. Codecs installed from the Microsoft Store will be used by Discord and other applications on the computer. </p><h2 id="h_01KG0M0A5ADZADCQQG0MQ7E72W">Getting The AVC Codec</h2><p>The AVC codec is required for core Discord functionality such as playing most file attachments and participating in video calls. An AVC encoder is especially important for camera and Go-Live screensharing. It is provided for free by Microsoft to Windows users.</p><p>To verify support, follow <a href="https://apps.microsoft.com/detail/9PB0TRCNRHFX?" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">this link</a> then press the View in Store button to launch the Microsoft Store app. </p><ul>
