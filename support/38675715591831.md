<p>Discord strictly prohibits non-consensual intimate imagery (NCII) and works to identify, remove, and prevent its spread across the platform. This article covers how people in the United States can file a non-consensual intimate imagery report under the <a href="https://www.congress.gov/119/plaws/publ12/PLAW-119publ12.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Take It Down Act</a> (TIDA), one of several tools available for the removal of NCII on Discord.</p><div class="info-box">
<strong>Note:</strong> Individuals who are not able to make a report under TIDA may submit reports of NCII through our standard <a href="https://discord.com/safety/360044103651-reporting-abusive-behavior-to-discord" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">in-app reporting system</a>. Our non-consensual intimate imagery <a href="https://discord.com/safety/non-consensual-intimate-media-policy-explainer" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">policy</a> applies globally in addition to other legal rights you may have where you live.</div><div class="toc-box">

<strong>Important:</strong> Need immediate help? If you have concerns about your personal safety, or the safety of someone you know, please contact local law enforcement or social services.</div><h1 id="h_01KN36BPD89BP2W555FFP1P8CX">What Can Be Reported</h1><p>If you are in the United States you can report content under TIDA that:</p><ul>
<li data-list-item-id="eb68003c0f21e748d9e910bb36d3bf936">Shows you (or someone you're authorized to represent) nude or engaged in sexually explicit conduct</li>
<li data-list-item-id="e5f792597663870fc7a0774d2b1a055ac">Was shared without your consent, or was captured when you had a reasonable expectation of privacy</li>
<li data-list-item-id="ea4e737e5d1c6815d10a72f5529e25a2e">Is either authentic, AI-generated, or digitally manipulated depicting you</li>
</ul><p>This includes images or videos you may have originally consented to create but did not consent to have shared. There's no time limit—you can report content regardless of when it was created or posted.</p><div class="warn-box">
<strong>Important:</strong> Please note that intimate imagery of a person under the age of 18 constitutes Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM). We report illegal child sexual abuse material and grooming to the National Center for Missing &amp; Exploited Children (NCMEC). Users who post this content are permanently banned from Discord.</div><h1 id="h_01KN36EW7CHQ1AA0YCMZCCMH7B">How to Submit a Report</h1><p>Reports under TIDA must be submitted through our public report webform found at <a href="http://discord.com/report" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">discord.com/report</a>.</p><p>Within 48 hours of a report being submitted, we’ll review the report and take appropriate action. You will be notified via email on the status of your report.</p><p>Reports may be submitted by the depicted individual or an authorized representative (e.g. a parent, guardian, attorney) of the depicted individual. Whichever approach you use, your report will be reviewed.</p><p><strong>Q: How quickly will the content be taken down after I report it?</strong></p><p>A: Within 48 hours of a report being submitted, we will review the report and will remove the content if it meets the criteria for removal under the Take It Down Act.</p><p><strong>Q: If an image is found to meet the criteria for removal under the Take It Down Act and is removed, can Discord prevent the same image from being uploaded again?</strong></p><p>A: Yes, once an image is found to meet the criteria for removal under the Take It Down Act, it will be tagged in our systems and we will prevent it from being uploaded again to the best of our ability.</p><p><strong>Q: Does Discord disclose my information to the person who shared the content that I report?</strong></p><p>A: No. We will not share the information you provide to us via the report form with users who posted the content to Discord. They will only be notified that the content has been removed, but not who reported it.</p><p><strong>Q: How will I know what happened to my report?</strong></p><p>A: After submitting your report, you'll hear from us twice. First, a confirmation email so you know your report reached us. Then, within 48 hours, we'll send a follow-up email with an update on whether the content was removed based on whether it met the criteria under the Take It Down Act.</p><p><strong>Q: Can I submit an appeal if action was not taken on my report?</strong></p><p>A: You have the option to submit another report if you believe we did not take appropriate action on your initial report. </p><p><strong>Q: How can I appeal if action is taken on my account?</strong></p><p>A: If you posted content that was found to meet the criteria for removal under the Take It Down Act or our Community Guidelines, you may be entitled to an appeal under your local laws. See our blog post with instructions on how to appeal <a href="https://discord.com/safety/360043712172-how-you-can-appeal-our-actions" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">here</a>.</p><p><strong>Q: What resources are available for me if my sensitive photos have been shared or published online?</strong></p><p>A: If your sensitive photos have been shared or published online, there is help available, including the following resources:</p><p><a href="https://stopncii.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">StopNCII</a> is a free tool designed to support adult victims and survivors of NCII abuse.</p><p><a href="https://takeitdown.ncmec.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Take It Down</a> is a free tool designed to support removal of online nude, partially nude, or sexually explicit imagery taken before the depicted person was 18. </p><p>More resources are also available in our <a href="https://discord.com/safety-wellbeing" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Wellbeing Hub</a>.</p>
