<li data-list-item-id="ef542c0ddd6f59e098be546ea2eb56d88">

</ul><h2 id="h_01KGJF023MFDQYH2G70HDK4JZR"><strong>Frequently Asked Questions</strong></h2><p><strong>Q: How do I know if my current version supports end-to-end encryption?</strong></p><p>A: If your call is end-to-end encrypted, you'll see a green lock icon labeled "End-to-end encrypted" in the Voice/Video Details panel. If you don't see this icon, you or another participant may be on an unsupported version. See our article on<a href="https://support.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/25968222946071" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"> <span class="wysiwyg-underline">End-to-End Encryption for Audio and Video</span></a> for more details.</p><p><strong>Q: What about console clients (PlayStation, Xbox)?</strong></p><p>A: Consoles generally require you to be on the latest system version which will include support for end-to-end encryption.</p><p><strong>Q: I use a third-party client or bot that connects to voice. Will it still work?</strong></p><p>A: Third-party applications and bots that connect to Discord voice must implement DAVE support to continue functioning after March 2, 2026. Developers should consult Discord's updated voice documentation and the open-source DAVE implementation examples.</p><p><strong>Q: Where can I learn more about end-to-end encryption on Discord?</strong></p><p>A: See our article on<a href="https://support.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/25968222946071" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"> <span class="wysiwyg-underline">End-to-End Encryption for Audio and Video</span></a> for details on how encryption works, how to verify calls, and how to manage your verification keys.</p>
