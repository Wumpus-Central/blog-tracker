<h1 id="h_01JVZE2KM50D0E365KMN8QBPF2">How to Keep Your Discord Account Safe</h1>
<p>To keep your Discord account safe, always avoid clicking on links or downloading files from unknown or untrusted sources. Malicious links and downloads can compromise your account and personal data. Additionally, enable <a href="https://support.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/219576828-Setting-up-Multi-Factor-Authentication"><span class="wysiwyg-underline" style="color: #1155cc;" data-darkreader-inline-color="">Multi-Factor Authentication</span></a>, use a strong, unique password, and be cautious of unsolicited messages—especially those requesting personal information. Staying vigilant and following these security practices will help keep your account protected.</p>
<p>For more information on how to keep your account safe, visit our<a href="https://discord.com/safety"> <span class="wysiwyg-underline" style="color: #1155cc;" data-darkreader-inline-color="">Discord Safety Center</span></a> to read these articles below:</p>

<h1 id="h_01JVZE2KM5YGBMME52VPGDXS2J">What to Do if You Suspect Your Discord Account Has Been Hacked or Compromised</h1>
<p>If you’re concerned about the security of your account, a good first step is to<a href="https://support.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/218410947-I-forgot-my-Password-Where-can-I-set-a-new-one"> <span class="wysiwyg-underline" style="color: #1155cc;" data-darkreader-inline-color="">reset your password</span></a> and ensure that <a href="https://support.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/219576828-Setting-up-Multi-Factor-Authentication" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Multi-Factor Authentication</a> is enabled. </p>
<p>Review your Authorized Apps and remove any unwanted applications that have access to your account:</p>
<p>1. On desktop/browser, tap on the <strong>cogwheel </strong>icon [<img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/32331211229463" width="24" height="24">] in the lower-left corner of the app to access your <strong>User Settings</strong>.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><span style="color: #2e3338;" data-darkreader-inline-color=""><img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/32331211230999" width="424" height="94"></span></p>
<h4 id="h_01JVZE91T3Z750FJY82QW2967F" class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><span style="color: #2e3338;" data-darkreader-inline-color=""><span style="color: #666666;" data-darkreader-inline-color="">Cogwheel icon</span></span></h4>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center">2. Tap on <strong>Authorized Apps. </strong>Here you can deauthorize any apps that are unwanted by pressing on <strong>Deauthorize</strong>.<img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/32331211234199" width="624" height="467"> </p>
<h4 id="h_01JVZE8RCQXWQ67PP9DEVX12DT" class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><span style="color: #666666;" data-darkreader-inline-color="">Deauthorize button</span></h4>
<p>3. If you’re on mobile, tap on your avatar in the bottom-right corner and then select the <strong>cogwheel </strong>icon [<img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/32331211229463" width="24" height="24">] at the top-right.</p>
<p>4. Tap on <strong>Authorized Apps</strong> and remove any you don’t recognize by tapping on the <strong>x button</strong>.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><strong><img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/32331224369943" width="409" height="888"></strong></p>
<h4 id="h_01JVZE8EBTMR5Z58B0M6FK3R91" class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><span style="color: #666666;" data-darkreader-inline-color="">Authorized Apps on mobile</span></h4>
<p>Finally, to ensure your device's security, we recommend running a Windows defender scan if you’re on Windows to check for any potential viruses.</p>
<p>For your protection, be mindful of the most common types of security threats:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Suspicious Links or Files</strong> – These may appear as “games” or promises of “free Nitro” but are often designed to steal your login credentials.</li>
<li>
<strong>Social Engineering</strong> – This involves individuals attempting to manipulate you into sharing your login, confidential, or personal information, which can then be used for fraudulent activities or to target other users.
<ul>
<li>Examples include scammers impersonating Discord employees offering to "appeal actions on your account" or asking for your credit card details to help you “win a giveaway.”</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<h1 id="h_01JVZE2KM5D5AMFD0YERP1ZPDD">How to Report Abusive Behavior on Discord</h1>
<p>When you encounter abusive behavior, please submit a report through the Discord app. Here’s how:</p>
<p>1. Select the Message you wish to report. On mobile, hold down on the Message, and on desktop/browser, “right-click.”</p>
<p>2. Select <strong>Report Message.</strong></p>
<p>3. Select the type of abuse you’re seeing.</p>
<p>You can learn more about how to send reports to Discord (including how to report user profiles) through <a href="https://discord.com/safety/360044103651-reporting-abusive-behavior-to-discord" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">here</a>. Additionally, you can learn about customizing your Discord experience and avoiding unwanted interactions in our <a href="https://discord.com/safetycenter" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Safety Center</a>.</p>
