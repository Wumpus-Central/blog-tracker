</div><h1 id="h_01JYHTWPE5V4JEPTGQ4A5ZY1KD">Discord Voice Troubleshooting</h1><p>If you're experiencing issues with voice chat on Discord, follow this step-by-step guide to help diagnose and resolve common problems.</p><p><strong>1. Check Your Microphone and Headset</strong></p><ul>
<li data-list-item-id="e52cc371206fec2f85a5ffe5689134bcd">Ensure your microphone or headset is properly connected to your device.</li>
<li data-list-item-id="e486af9c598eaa6430074b368992322b2">If using a USB microphone, try plugging it into a different port.</li>
<li data-list-item-id="e438e7d6f85d25e804d84b46010cb8e0d">Test your microphone with another app to confirm it’s working.</li>
</ul><p><strong>2. Verify Input and Output Devices in Discord</strong></p><p>2a. To check your input devices through the desktop/browser app, tap on the <strong>cogwheel </strong>icon<strong> </strong>[<img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/33030151277719" width="24" height="24">]<strong> </strong>in the lower-left corner of the app to access your <strong>User Settings</strong>.</p><p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center">
    <span style="color: #2e3338;"><img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/33030164083735" width="424" height="94"></span>
  </p><h4 class="wysiwyg-text-align-center" id="h_01JYHTWPEB8YNVFH7FJZSPQ8QP"><span style="color: #666666;">Cogwheel icon on Discord desktop/browser</span></h4><p>2b. Tap on the <strong>Voice &amp; Video</strong> tab. Make sure the correct <strong>Input Device</strong> and <strong>Output Device</strong> are selected. If you’re having trouble with your microphone not working while using the Discord app on desktop/browser, you can also run a <a href="https://support.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/360020641332" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span class="wysiwyg-underline">Mic Test</span></a>.</p><p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/33030151279767" width="589" height="456"></p><h4 class="wysiwyg-text-align-center" id="h_01JYHTWPEE7B4ZAE846ADA5J63"><span style="color: #666666;">Example of the Input and Outside Device settings under the Voice &amp; Video tab</span></h4><p>2c. On the Discord mobile app, tap on your avatar in the bottom-right corner. Then tap on the cogwheel in the top-right corner to access <strong>User Settings</strong>.</p><p>2d. While under <strong>User Settings, </strong>scroll down and tap on <strong>Voice</strong> under <strong>App Settings</strong>. Here you can check your Input settings to ensure the correct <strong>Input Mode</strong> is selected (<strong>Voice Activity</strong> or P<strong>ush to Talk</strong>).</p><p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/33030164086551" width="374" height="809"></p><h4 class="wysiwyg-text-align-center" id="h_01JYHTWPEGND1JE60AWN14NPE2"><span style="color: #666666;">Where to find Voice settings on mobile</span></h4><p>2e. On the mobile app, you can adjust the Output volume and see if enabling or disabled additional settings helps.</p><p><strong>3. Make sure you’re not Muted or Deafened</strong></p><p>Double-check to ensure you haven’t accidentally muted or deafened yourself. You can check this on desktop/browser by looking to the right-hand side of your avatar in the bottom-left corner of the app.</p><p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/33030151281431" width="293" height="61"></p><h4 class="wysiwyg-text-align-center" id="h_01JYHTWPEHXZ1A7HMVHMNY6MBK"><span style="color: #666666;">Example of what it looks like when you are muted and deafened in Discord on the desktop app/browser</span></h4><p><strong>4. Check for any Discord app updates</strong></p><p>Ensure you're using the latest version of the app, whether on desktop or mobile, to maintain compatibility and access the latest features.</p><h2 id="h_01JYHTWPEJY8CA2RF05Y9DASZ5">Voice Troubleshooting Additional Resources</h2><p>If you’re still running into issues with voice, here are some additional resources that can assist you with further troubleshooting and other features you can learn more about.</p><ul>

</ul><h1 id="h_01JYHTWPEPVC1DQ4TGCCVS7D4H">Discord Video Troubleshooting</h1><p>If you're experiencing issues with sharing your video or viewing someone else's on Discord, this guide will help you get started with some troubleshooting steps.</p><p><strong>1. Check Video device settings</strong></p><p>1a. If your video is not displaying for others, the first thing you want to check is that the correct camera device is selected. </p><p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/33030164087831" width="624" height="481"></p><h4 class="wysiwyg-text-align-center" id="h_01JYHTWPEQ1G2TBH2Z8ZDKDKT1"><span style="color: #666666;">Where to locate your camera settings under Voice &amp; Video tab</span></h4><p>1b. If the correct device is selected, please double-check the following:</p><ul>
<li data-list-item-id="e19c598144e50ad23aedd97ae780dd8b4">Ensure your camera is properly connected to your device and try another port.</li>
<li data-list-item-id="ea5c8bbfeb3ad1a7c4180d9fdbecdab0d">If using an external webcam, try unplugging and reconnecting it.</li>
</ul><p>Test the camera in another application to confirm it’s working.</p><p><strong>2. Check for any updates</strong></p><p>Ensure you're using the latest version of the app, whether on desktop or mobile, to maintain compatibility and access the latest features.</p><p>If you have software installed for your webcam, check to make sure it doesn’t have any outstanding updates as well.</p><h2 id="h_01JYHTWPERV2390W467C5P10G1">Video Troubleshooting Additional Resources</h2><p>If you’re still running into issues with Discord video, here are some additional resources that can not only assist you with further troubleshooting, but also allow you to learn more about additional features.</p><ul>

</ul><h1 id="h_01JYHTWPEVMMX7CWCS568BR60B">Discord Streaming &amp; Screen Share Troubleshooting</h1><p>If you’re running into any issues with screen sharing or streaming, here are some settings to check.</p><p><strong>1. Check your Internet Connection</strong></p><ul>
<li data-list-item-id="e5d2779edd3acb3c319b1ecfcbbd59b16">Ensure you have a stable internet connection to prevent lag or disconnections.</li>
<li data-list-item-id="e341e0c2d69f723eee2e8397612a65b1a">If possible, use a wired connection for better stability.</li>
</ul><p><strong>2. Restart Discord and Your Device &amp; Check for Updates</strong></p><ul>
<li data-list-item-id="e31a7ac4d3cf6035d2be32cfa99f23e6a">Close and reopen Discord to refresh the app.</li>
<li data-list-item-id="e629a87ba49b77ed6f30916c7a78b5ef2">If the issue persists, restart your computer or mobile device.</li>
<li data-list-item-id="ee3bbad561028d0e7b446c6e3263c9965">Make sure you’re also running the latest version of the Discord app.</li>
</ul><p><strong>3. Disable Hardware Acceleration</strong></p><p>If you have <strong>Hardware Acceleration</strong> on, and you find your stream is lagging, please try disabling this setting to see if it helps. </p><p>3a. Tap on the <strong>cogwheel </strong>icon<strong> </strong>[<img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/33030151277719" width="24" height="24">]<strong> </strong>in the lower-left corner of the app to access your <strong>User Settings</strong> in the desktop/browser app.</p><p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center">
    <span style="color: #2e3338;"><img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/33030164083735" width="424" height="94"></span>
  </p><h4 class="wysiwyg-text-align-center" id="h_01JYHTWPEZ57QN9TM2H1B2870A"><span style="color: #666666;">Cogwheel icon on Discord desktop/browser</span></h4><p>3b. Press on <strong>Voice &amp; Video </strong>then tap on the <strong>Video</strong> tab. While here, scroll down until you see the <strong>Hardware Acceleration</strong> setting. If enabled, tap on the check mark to disable. </p><p class="wysiwyg-text-align-center"><img src="https://support.discord.com/hc/article_attachments/33030151283863" width="624" height="476"></p><h4 class="wysiwyg-text-align-center" id="h_01JYHTWPF08NZ52QB0C3Q0J7VB"><span style="color: #666666;">Example of where the Hardware Acceleration setting is located under the Voice &amp; Video tab</span></h4><p><strong>4. Grant Necessary Permissions</strong></p><p>Make sure that your desktop device has all of the necessary permissions to be able to screen share in Discord.</p><p>Windows:</p><ul>
<li data-list-item-id="e70e1c7855ea633e69a31f1eed538f0fd">Run Discord as an administrator by right-clicking the Discord icon and selecting <strong>Run as administrator</strong>.</li>
<li data-list-item-id="ef9dcaf3057fcea5ba8b7b23009b09383">Check <strong>Settings &gt; Privacy &gt; Screen Recording</strong> to ensure Discord has permission to record the screen.</li>
</ul><p>macOS:</p><ul>
<li data-list-item-id="e0dd142b4d2a7d6168f3830bfa9200cda">Go to <strong>System Preferences &gt; Security &amp; Privacy &gt; Privacy</strong>.</li>
<li data-list-item-id="ed3331159d26e085fea34ca008b1a80b6">Under <strong>Screen Recording</strong>, ensure Discord is checked.</li>
